What is the network load and how to increase the transaction rate?

Network load is the set of transactions in a blockchain that are waiting to be confirmed due to low fees, increased load and other factors. The system is kept running by miners, who use processing power to process transactions and find the necessary hash for the transaction. Transactions with a higher commission are processed first, then miners are accepted for transactions with a lower premium. That's how the queue is formed, with the more profitable transactions in the "head" and the payments with the minimum premium in the "tail".If the sender needs a fast transfer, he sets a higher premium, which ensures that the transaction is included in the block without delays. With savings on fees, a new transaction can take up to 3 days or more. When you create a request on our website, you will be able to see the recommended network fee. This commission will increase the chance of a transaction being confirmed soon.

What are Tag and Memo?

MEMO is a special identifier used in XRP transactions. In Stellar, the target tag is called memo. Many services, including Freewallet, have one XRP and XLM address for all customers. The only difference is a special set of numbers, called a destination tag, unique to each user. When you are asked to enter a tag or note, do not ignore this action! Otherwise, your funds will be lost and sent to the general balance of the platform, but not to your account. Imagine the tag as the number of your house on a long street. You need to know the exact building number in order for the package to reach its final destination. The same goes for these coins. If you use a personal wallet, using TAG or MEMO is optional. In case you deposit funds on the exchange, TAG or MEMO is mandatory.
